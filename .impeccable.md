# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Two groups sharing the same system:
- **Accountants / Finance staff** — daily power users, process batches of invoices, familiar with accounting software conventions, primarily desktop, need density and efficiency
- **SME business owners** — occasional users, check Dashboard, approve invoices, may be on mobile; want simplicity and confidence that everything is in order
- **LINE-first operators** — owners, admins, and field staff who send slips, bills, and delivery evidence from their phone. They do not want to learn accounting software before the document is captured.

Both groups operate in a **formal Thai business context** — submissions go to the Revenue Department (กรมสรรพากร). The stakes feel real: errors cost money and time.

### Competitive Goal
Billboy should not try to beat FlowAccount, PEAK, and Paypers by having the longest feature list. It should beat them by making the most common SME loop feel faster and safer:

1. Capture a bill, slip, invoice, or delivery proof from LINE or the web.
2. Turn it into an accounting-ready record with minimal correction.
3. Send a customer-facing link with payment instructions.
4. Show the owner what is paid, unpaid, missing, and safe for tax.

North-star promise: **from document/photo/chat to paid or tax-ready record in under 3 minutes**.

### Brand Personality
**Clear · Trustworthy · Professional**

The interface should feel like a well-run law firm's software — nothing flashy, nothing confusing, everything precisely where it should be. Users should feel *calm and in control*, not excited or entertained.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode — used in offices during the day, formal context, government submissions
- **Color**: Navy blue primary (deeper, more authoritative than current Tailwind blue-500). Shift from `#2563eb` toward `#1e3a8a` zone. Clean white backgrounds.
- **Anti-references**: No SaaS startup purple/gradient aesthetic. No "AI slop" hero metrics with glowing accents. Should NOT look like a marketing site.
- **References**: Thai Banking apps (KBank, SCB Biz), Notion, Linear — clean information density without decoration.

### Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — accountants scan tables, not admire cards. Tighten row spacing, reduce decorative padding.
2. **Trust through restraint** — no gradients on functional buttons. Solid fills only. Color means something.
3. **Clarity above all** — every label, badge, and status should be unambiguous even under time pressure.
4. **Bilingual as first-class** — Thai and English text must both look deliberate, not like an afterthought.
5. **Mobile-aware, desktop-first** — SME owners check on phones, accountants work on desktops. Core flows must work on both.
6. **Next action always visible** — dashboards and lists should answer "what should I do now?" without forcing users to inspect every menu.
7. **Customer surfaces are simpler than seller surfaces** — public invoice links and customer portal pages should focus on amount, due date, status, PDF, PromptPay, and contact path only.
8. **LINE is an input channel, not a side feature** — OCR, slip matching, save-as-expense, and review/edit should feel like a first-class workflow connected to the web app.

### Product UX Anti-Goals
- Do not leak engineering or tax-infrastructure terms into customer or owner-facing UI: no tenant, backend endpoint, XAdES, BullMQ, magic link, webhook, or worker copy outside admin/debug surfaces.
- Do not create generic SaaS marketing pages for core product work. The first screen after login should be a usable workspace.
- Do not bury payment/share actions behind menus when an unpaid invoice is visible.
- Do not make the owner choose between many accounting concepts before creating the first invoice.
- Do not use AI/purple/glow styling to communicate intelligence. Intelligence should show through fewer corrections, clearer next steps, and saved time.

### Winning Flow Checklist
Every major UX change should improve at least one of these:

- First invoice created and shared in 3 minutes or less.
- LINE receipt/slip saved or matched in 30 seconds after OCR completes.
- Customer invoice link opens without login and makes payment action obvious.
- Owner dashboard shows money state first: unpaid, paid today, missing documents, tax-safe status.
- Mobile screen has the same primary action as desktop, with less chrome.
